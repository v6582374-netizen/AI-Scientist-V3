# Title: Formation and Evolution of Induced Magnetospheric Structures Around Unmagnetized Terrestrial Planets

## Keywords
planetary space physics, induced magnetosphere, Mars, Venus, solar wind, interplanetary magnetic field, ion dynamics, atmospheric escape, comparative planetology

## TL;DR
Study whether Mars-like and Venus-like induced magnetospheres share common formation and evolution mechanisms under changing solar wind and interplanetary magnetic field conditions, using CPU-friendly reduced numerical experiments and reproducible analysis rather than large-scale plasma simulations.

## Abstract
The formation mechanisms and evolution of near-planet space structures around terrestrial planets are central questions in planetary space physics. Earth has a global intrinsic magnetic field, so its magnetosphere is shaped by coupled magnetic-field and plasma interactions. Mars and Venus lack global intrinsic magnetic fields, so their near-planet structures are formed mainly through direct interaction between the solar wind and the upper atmosphere or ionosphere. This difference produces distinct magnetospheric configurations, particle dynamics, and atmospheric escape pathways.

This research theme asks whether induced magnetospheres around unmagnetized terrestrial planets show comparable structural behavior, how ion-scale dynamics influence particle acceleration, transport, and escape, and whether common response patterns emerge under different solar wind driving conditions, including solar wind density, velocity, dynamic pressure, interplanetary magnetic field direction and strength, and solar activity cycle state.

For the first smoke-test run, proposals should favor simplified, executable, CPU-friendly evidence plans. Suitable approaches include reduced analytical models, low-dimensional numerical simulations, parameter sweeps, sensitivity analysis, dimensional analysis, synthetic-data experiments, or reproducible comparisons of idealized Mars-like and Venus-like boundary conditions. The goal is not to perform a full global hybrid, MHD, or particle-in-cell simulation. The generated experiments should produce interpretable figures or summary metrics that help evaluate structural comparability, response scaling, or escape-efficiency mechanisms under clearly stated assumptions.

## Research Questions
1. Do induced magnetospheric structures around unmagnetized terrestrial planets have comparable formation and evolution patterns?
2. At ion-dynamic scales, how do induced magnetospheric structures affect particle acceleration, transport, and atmospheric escape?
3. Under changing solar wind and interplanetary magnetic field conditions, do Mars-like and Venus-like induced magnetospheres exhibit shared response laws or scaling behavior?

## Smoke-Test Constraints
- Treat this as a non-machine-learning planetary space physics problem.
- Use executable code only for lightweight simulation, numerical analysis, parameter sweeps, plotting, or reproducibility checks.
- Prefer CPU execution and avoid requiring hardware accelerators, large datasets, or long-running global plasma simulations.
- Make claims proportional to limited local evidence; report assumptions and limitations explicitly.
